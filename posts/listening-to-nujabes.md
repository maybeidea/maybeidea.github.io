I write most of my first drafts to a Nujabes loop. Not because I particularly
want them to sound "like" Nujabes — I don't — but because the rhythm
**forces a sentence to finish on the four**.

## What that means

A four-bar phrase in a jazz-hip-hop loop is, in effect, a small time budget.
If your sentence is still going when the loop resets, the sentence is too
long. This is a surprisingly good editor.

> [!note] Small heuristic
> If a sentence outlasts a Nujabes phrase, it probably has two ideas in it
> and should be split.

## A partial list of loops I trust

- **"Aruarian Dance"** — for opening paragraphs.
- **"Luv(sic) pt. 3"** — for transitions.
- **"Feather"** — for anything that needs to end softly.

## The deeper point

Every writing tool is, fundamentally, a **pacing device**. A typewriter paces
you through resistance. A notebook paces you through the size of the line. A
loop paces you through the bar. What you choose determines how your prose
breathes.

That is all. Short note. End on the four.
